In the beginning.

An Englishman named Tim Barners-Lee invented and developed the World Wide Web throughout the year 1990.

By Christmas of that year he had built all the tools he needed to demonstrate his idea:

  • A server.
  • A browser.
  • And some web pages.
  • wikipedia.org/wiki/World_wide_web
  • commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Tim_Berners-Lee_CP.jpg

Tim's first Web server.

  • commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:First_Web_Server.jpg

Near death experience.

Tim's vision of the WWW was to collaborate on scientific projects with laboratories and universities around the World.

  • wikipedia.org/wiki/World_wide_web

The breakthrough.

Marc Andreesen, a student at U of Illinois, made one simple addition to Tim's HTML that changed the World forever.

<img src="" />

  • wikipedia.org/wiki/World_wide_web
  • commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Marc_Andreessen.jpg

This is where Al Gore comes in.

Marc and his friends called their web browser "Mosaic" and introduced it to the World in 1993.

The funding for Marc's web browser project at U of Illinois came from the High Performance Computing and Communication Act of 1991, one of several computing developments initiated by U.S. Senator Al Gore.

  • wikipedia.org/wiki/Mosaic_(web_browser)
  • commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Mosaic_browser_plaque_ncsa.jpg

And it was good.

After graduating in the Spring of 1994 Marc started a company called Netscape and their first product was...

Yup -- Netscape "Navigator". It was released on November 14th, 1994.

Netscape went public on August 9th, 1995 for $28 per share.

By the end of the day it was trading at $75 per share.

  • http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Netscape
  • http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:NASDAQ_IXIC_-_dot-com_bubble_small.png

But, trouble was brewing...

  • http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:The_Evil_Empire.text_drawing.jpg

His Billness

Bill realized he had made a big mistake by brushing off the WWW.

Microsoft licensed the Mosaic browser technology from the University of Illinois.

The irony was not lost on the folks at Netscape.

  • http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Bill_Gates_-_World_Economic_Forum_Annual_Meeting_New_York_2002.jpg

It's all fun and games until sombody goes out of business.

  • www.flickr.com/photos/phoneboy/2116016112/

Browser war.

Microsoft and Netscape added features at a rapid pace, each trying to out match the other.

Stupid HTML tags like <BLINK> and <MARQUEE> were added.

Bug fixes and security problems were ignored.

Standards compliance was thrown out the window.

  • http://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:SS-15_ICBM.JPEG

The nuclear option.

The Netscape crew still had a trick up their sleeve.

The birth of a language.

Marc Andreessen went out looking for a really smart guy to invent a programming language they could tie into the browser. He found Brendan Eich.

Brendan was indeed very smart.

  • http://www.flickr.com/photos/equanimity/4055148344/

The Manhattan project.

Brendan invented the new scripting language in 10 days.

LiveScript was included into the Netscape Navigator 2.0 browser in september of 1995.

Confusion.

There was another language called Java that was invented at Sun Microsystems around the same time.

So, LiveScript legally changed its name to JavaScript.

JavaScript actually has nothing to do with Java ... And don't forget it.

  • http://www.flickr.com/photos/equanimity/4055148344/

It gets worse before it gets better.

"His Billness" quickly realized the danger his fiefdom faced from the threat of JavaScript.

So, he locked them in the basement and and forced them to build an exact copy of JavaScript.

Feature for feature and bug for bug.

  • commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Explosions.jpg
  • toastytech.com/evil/onlyie.html

Things are not looking good.

JavaScript was scorned by professionals and dismissed by amateurs.

Fast forward to 2004.

  • www.flickr.com/photos/niallkennedy/3573459849/

JavaScript? Seriously?

Lars and Jens Rasmussen developed an online mapping tool during 2004, and on February 8th, 2005 it was released as Google Maps.

How the H - E double hocky sticks did they do that with a web page?
